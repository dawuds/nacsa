## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This procedure establishes the step-by-step process by which [Organization Name] fulfils its legal obligation to register as a National Critical Information Infrastructure (NCII) entity with the National Cyber Security Agency (NACSA) and the relevant sector lead, in accordance with the **Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854)**, specifically **Section 17** (duty to register) and **Section 57** (transitional provisions for existing operators).

The procedure ensures that all registration activities are conducted in a timely, accurate, and audit-ready manner, and that all required supporting documentation is prepared, reviewed, and submitted in compliance with NACSA's prescribed formats and timelines.

### 1.2 Scope

*This section defines which parts of the organization, systems, and processes are covered by this procedure. Update to reflect the organization's actual NCII sector classification and operational footprint.*

This procedure applies to:

- All departments and subsidiaries of [Organization Name] that own, operate, or manage systems or assets designated or likely to be designated as NCII under Act 854.
- The **[Compliance / Risk / IT Security]** team responsible for regulatory submissions.
- Third-party service providers engaged to support NCII operations, where relevant obligations are passed through contractual arrangements.

This procedure does **not** cover:

- Ongoing post-registration obligations (refer to **[Document ID — NCII Ongoing Compliance Procedure]**).
- Incident notification and reporting obligations under Act 854 (refer to **[Document ID — NCII Incident Notification Procedure]**).
- Cyber security measures compliance audits (refer to **[Document ID — NCII Cyber Security Measures Procedure]**).

### 1.3 Objectives

Upon completion of this procedure, [Organization Name] will have:

- [ ] Confirmed its status and eligibility as an NCII entity.
- [ ] Assembled all required documentation and asset inventory.
- [ ] Coordinated with the relevant sector lead.
- [ ] Submitted a complete registration application via the NACSA portal or prescribed channel.
- [ ] Retained evidence of submission and acknowledgement for audit purposes.

## 5. NCII Asset Inventory Preparation

*This section guides the preparation of the asset inventory that must be submitted as part of the registration. The inventory must accurately reflect all assets that are or may be designated as NCII. Consult NACSA's prescribed inventory template if one is provided.*

### 5.1 Asset Identification Methodology

[Organization Name] shall identify NCII assets using the following approach:

1. **Top-down mapping:** Start from critical business functions and services that, if disrupted, would have a significant national or sectoral impact.
2. **Bottom-up enumeration:** Enumerate underlying IT and OT systems, networks, and components that support those critical functions.
3. **Dependency analysis:** Identify upstream and downstream dependencies, including third-party and cloud-hosted components.
4. **Boundary definition:** Establish clear logical and physical boundaries for each NCII asset cluster.

### 5.3 Asset Classification Criteria

Assets shall be classified by criticality based on the following criteria:

| Criticality Level | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Critical** | Loss or compromise would immediately and directly result in a significant adverse impact on national security, economy, public health, or safety. |
| **High** | Loss or compromise would result in significant operational disruption to essential services within the sector, with potential cascading effects. |
| **Medium** | Loss or compromise would result in material operational disruption but with available workarounds and limited cross-sector impact. |

## 7. NACSA Portal Submission Steps

*This section provides detailed, step-by-step instructions for completing the registration submission via the NACSA portal (or alternative prescribed channel). Update these steps if NACSA modifies the portal interface or submission process.*

### 7.1 Portal Access and Account Setup

**Step 1 — Access the NACSA Registration Portal**

- Navigate to the official NACSA portal at **[NACSA Portal URL — confirm from NACSA official communications]**.
- Ensure access is made from a secure, organization-managed device.
- Do not access the portal from personal devices or unsecured networks.

**Step 2 — Create or Log In to the Organization Account**

- If [Organization Name] does not yet have a portal account, select **"Register New Organization"** and provide:
  - Legal entity name (as registered with SSM or equivalent authority)
  - Company registration number
  - Sector classification
  - Primary contact details (NCII Registration Lead)
- If an account exists, log in using the credentials established in Section 4.1 (Pre-Registration Checklist, item 2.5).
- Record account credentials in the organization's approved password management system.

**Step 3 — Verify Account and Receive Confirmation**

- Upon account creation, NACSA may send a verification email to the registered contact.
- Complete email verification within the prescribed timeframe.
- Retain the account confirmation email as part of the registration audit file.

### 7.2 Completing the Registration Form

**Step 4 — Initiate a New Registration Application**

- From the portal dashboard, select **"New NCII Entity Registration"** (or equivalent menu item).
- Note the application reference number generated by the system; record this in the Registration Log (Appendix B).

**Step 5 — Section A: Organization Information**

Complete the following fields as prompted by the portal:

| Field | Information to Enter |
|---|---|
| Legal Entity Name | [Organization Name] — as per SSM registration |
| SSM / BRN Number | [Registration Number] |
| Registered Address | [Registered Business Address] |
| Primary Business Activity | [Description — align with MSIC code] |
| NCII Sector | [Select from portal dropdown — confirm correct sector] |
| Number of Employees | [Approximate headcount] |
| Annual Turnover (if required) | [MYR — refer to Finance] |

**Step 6 — Section B: NCII Asset Declaration**

- Upload the completed **NCII Asset Register** (prepared in Section 5).
- Ensure the file format matches NACSA's requirements (e.g., Excel .xlsx, PDF).
- For each asset declared, complete the asset description fields as prompted.
- Confirm whether the asset is solely owned, jointly owned, or operated under a third-party arrangement.

**Step 7 — Section C: Sector Lead Information**

- Enter the name of the sector lead agency.
- Upload the sector lead endorsement or pre-registration acknowledgement obtained in Section 6.
- If the sector lead endorsement is pending at the time of portal submission, note this in the remarks field and upload as a subsequent amendment.

**Step 8 — Section D: Key Personnel Declaration**

Declare the following personnel as required by the portal:

| Role | Name | IC / Passport No. | Designation | Contact |

**Step 9 — Section E: Supporting Documentation Upload**

- Upload all supporting documents as specified in **Section 8** of this procedure.
- Verify that each document is legible, complete, and correctly labelled before upload.
- Confirm file sizes and formats comply with portal limitations.

**Step 10 — Declaration and Submission**

- Ensure the authorized signatory (as identified in Section 10) reviews and approves the application before final submission.
