## 1. Purpose

*State the primary objective of this procedure, the problem it addresses, and the regulatory obligation it fulfils. Link explicitly to the Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854) Section 18 obligations on cyber security awareness.*

This procedure establishes a structured, repeatable process for planning, delivering, and measuring the effectiveness of cyber security awareness training across [Organization Name]. It ensures that all personnel — including permanent employees, contractors, and third-party users with access to organizational information systems — maintain an appropriate level of cyber security awareness commensurate with their roles and the sensitivity of the systems and data they access.

This procedure fulfils the obligations imposed on **National Critical Information Infrastructure (NCII) entity operators** under **Section 18 of the Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854)**, which requires entities to implement measures to promote cyber security awareness among their personnel. Compliance with this procedure also supports [Organization Name]'s obligations under:

- **Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy** — Section on Cyber Security Awareness
- **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)** — Principle 7: Security
- **[Organization Name] Information Security Policy** — [Policy Reference]

## 9. Phishing Simulation Campaigns

*Phishing simulations are a key tool for measuring real-world susceptibility and driving behavioural change. This section governs the planning, execution, and follow-up for all phishing simulation activities.*

### 9.1 Campaign Planning

The Information Security Team shall plan each phishing simulation campaign at least **[4 weeks]** in advance. Planning shall include:

- **Scenario selection** — Simulation templates shall reflect current phishing trends observed in Malaysia and the broader financial services sector (sourced from NACSA advisories, BNM MyCERT, or the simulation platform's threat library)
- **Target population** — Campaigns shall target [all staff / defined staff cohorts as specified in the campaign plan]
- **Timing** — Campaigns shall not be scheduled during known peak operational periods (e.g., financial year-end, regulatory submission deadlines) unless specifically testing readiness during high-stress periods
- **Exclusions** — Any staff excluded from a specific campaign must be documented with justification and approved by the CISO
- **Pre-notification** — No advance warning of campaign timing shall be given to participants. The existence of the phishing simulation programme shall be disclosed as part of general awareness training (but not specific campaign timing)

### 9.2 Campaign Execution

| Step | Action | Responsible | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Finalise campaign scenario and target list | InfoSec Team | T-14 days |
| 2 | Configure simulation platform (sender domain, template, landing page) | InfoSec Team | T-7 days |
| 3 | Obtain CISO written approval for campaign launch | CISO | T-5 days |
| 4 | Launch campaign | InfoSec Team | Campaign start date (T=0) |
| 5 | Monitor real-time dashboard during active campaign window | InfoSec Team | T=0 to T+[X days] |
| 6 | Close campaign and extract raw results | InfoSec Team | T+[X days] |
| 7 | Assign targeted remediation training to clickers | InfoSec Team / HR | Within 5 business days of close |
| 8 | Prepare and distribute campaign results report | InfoSec Team | Within 10 business days of close |
| 9 | Brief CISO and HR on results | InfoSec / CISO | Within 15 business days of close |

### 9.3 Metrics and Targets

*The following metrics shall be tracked for every phishing simulation campaign. Targets shall be reviewed annually and updated to reflect programme maturity.*

| Metric | Definition | Current Target | Reporting Period |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Open Rate** | % of recipients who opened the simulated email | Tracking only | Per campaign |
| **Click Rate** | % of recipients who clicked the simulated link or attachment | < [e.g., 10%] | Per campaign |
| **Credential Submission Rate** | % of recipients who submitted data on a simulated credential harvest page | < [e.g., 5%] | Per campaign |
| **Report Rate** | % of recipients who reported the simulated phishing email | > [e.g., 30%] | Per campaign |
| **Repeat Clicker Rate** | % of staff who clicked in two or more consecutive campaigns | < [e.g., 3%] | Rolling annual |

### 9.4 Post-Campaign Response

Upon conclusion of each campaign:

1. **All participants** shall receive an educational debrief notification (via email or LMS notification) explaining the simulation, what the phishing indicators were, and how to correctly handle such emails
2. **Staff who clicked** shall be automatically enrolled in targeted remediation training module **[AWR-003 or equivalent]** and notified via email and LMS
3. **Repeat clickers** (staff who clicked in two or more consecutive campaigns) shall be subject to escalation as defined in Section 12 (Non-Compliance and Escalation)
4. **Staff who correctly reported** the simulation shall receive a positive acknowledgement communication

## 11. Effectiveness Measurement and Reporting

*Measuring the effectiveness of the awareness programme is essential to demonstrate regulatory compliance and to drive continuous improvement. This section establishes how effectiveness is defined, measured, and reported.*

### 11.1 Effectiveness Framework

Effectiveness shall be measured across four dimensions adapted from the **Kirkpatrick Model**:

| Level | Dimension | Measurement Approach | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | Reaction — Did learners find the training relevant and engaging? | Post-module satisfaction surveys | Per module delivery |
| **Level 2** | Learning — Did learners acquire the intended knowledge? | Pre/post assessment scores; knowledge check pass rates | Per module delivery |
| **Level 3** | Behaviour — Are learners applying what they learned? | Phishing simulation click and report rates; security incident trends | Bi-annual (simulation) + ongoing |
| **Level 4** | Impact — Has the programme reduced organizational risk? | Security incident metrics; audit findings; regulatory feedback | Annual |

### 11.2 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

*The following KPIs shall be tracked and reported annually to Senior Management and the Board. Targets shall be set at the beginning of each programme year and reviewed annually.*

| KPI | Description | Target | Measurement Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Completion Rate** | % of assigned staff who complete mandatory training within the deadline | ≥ [95%] | LMS |
| **Assessment Pass Rate** | % of learners who pass module knowledge checks on first attempt | ≥ [85%] | LMS |
| **Learner Satisfaction Score** | Average post-module satisfaction rating (1–5 scale) | ≥ [4.0 / 5.0] | Post-module survey |
| **Phishing Click Rate (Year-on-Year)** | Change in phishing simulation click rate versus prior year | Decreasing trend / < [10%] | Simulation platform |
| **Phishing Report Rate (Year-on-Year)** | Change in phishing simulation report rate versus prior year | Increasing trend / > [30%] | Simulation platform |
| **Security Incident — Human Factor %** | % of security incidents attributable to human error or social engineering | Decreasing trend | Incident Register |
| **Repeat Clicker Rate** | % of staff who clicked in two or more consecutive phishing simulations | < [3%] | Simulation platform |
| **Regulatory Findings** | Number of awareness-related findings raised in BNM / NACSA / Internal Audit | Zero findings | Audit reports |

### 11.3 Annual Effectiveness Review

The Information Security Team shall conduct a formal **Annual Effectiveness Review** of the awareness programme. The review shall:

1. Consolidate all KPI data from the preceding programme year
2. Benchmark results against prior years to identify trends
3. Analyse qualitative feedback from learner surveys and post-training focus groups
4. Review security incident data for patterns attributable to human factors
5. Assess whether the programme content remains aligned with the current threat landscape
6. Identify gaps, areas for improvement, and proposed changes to the next-year programme
7. Document findings in the **Annual Awareness Programme Effectiveness Report** (see Appendix D for template)

The Annual Effectiveness Review Report shall be:
- Completed by **[Month]** each year
- Reviewed by HR/L&D and the Compliance function
- Approved by the CISO
- Presented to **[Board Risk Committee / Senior Management Cyber Risk Committee]** as part of the annual cyber security reporting cycle

## 12. Non-Compliance and Escalation

*This section defines the consequences of non-compliance with mandatory training requirements and the escalation pathway for persistent non-compliance.*

### 12.1 Non-Compliance Definition

An employee is considered **non-compliant** with this procedure when they:

- Fail to complete a mandatory training module by the specified deadline
- Fail the remediation module following a phishing simulation click (after the maximum permitted attempts)
- Deliberately circumvent LMS completion tracking (e.g., clicking through without engaging)
- Are identified as a **repeat clicker** in phishing simulations on two or more consecutive occasions

### 12.2 Consequences of Non-Compliance

Non-compliance with mandatory cyber security training is treated as a **disciplinary matter** in accordance with [Organization Name]'s HR Disciplinary Policy ([HR Policy Reference]).

| Non-Compliance Stage | Action |
|---|---|
| First instance of non-completion past deadline | Written reminder; formal record in HR training file |
| Continued non-completion (>14 days past deadline) | Formal written warning issued by HR in consultation with line manager |
| Persistent non-completion (>21 days) | Escalation to CISO and HR Business Partner; consideration of formal disciplinary proceedings |
| Deliberate circumvention of training | Immediate escalation to HR for disciplinary action regardless of timeline |
| Repeat phishing clicker (3+ consecutive campaigns) | Mandatory 1-on-1 security coaching session; review of user access privileges; formal record in HR file |

### 12.3 Exceptions

Exceptions to mandatory training completion deadlines may be granted in limited circumstances (e.g., extended medical leave, parental leave). All exceptions must be:

- Requested by the employee's line manager
- Approved by HR in consultation with the CISO
- Documented in the **Training Exception Register** (Appendix E)
- Subject to a rescheduled completion deadline upon the employee's return
