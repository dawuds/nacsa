## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This register serves as the authoritative compliance tracking instrument for all directives issued by the Chief Executive of the National Cyber Security Agency (NACSA) under **Section 15 of the Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854)**. It provides [Organization Name] with a structured mechanism to record, monitor, and evidence compliance with each directive, ensuring accountability and audit readiness.

### 1.2 Scope

This document applies to all directives issued to [Organization Name] as a designated Critical Information Infrastructure (CII) entity or regulated entity under Act 854. It encompasses:

- All binding directives received from the NACSA Chief Executive under s15
- Directives relating to incident response, risk mitigation, security controls, and operational changes
- Internal compliance activities, remediation tasks, and evidence collection arising from each directive
- All departments, business units, and third-party service providers subject to a given directive

### 1.3 Regulatory Basis

This register is maintained in accordance with:

- **Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854), Section 15** — Powers of the Chief Executive to issue directives to entities
- Any subsidiary legislation, codes of practice, or guidelines issued pursuant to Act 854
- [Organization Name]'s internal Cyber Security Policy and Compliance Management Framework

## 6. Evidence Reference

*Maintain a comprehensive index of all documentary and technical evidence supporting compliance with each directive requirement. Evidence must be sufficient to satisfy NACSA audit and inspection requirements under Act 854.*

### 6.1 Acceptable Evidence Types

*The following evidence types are recognised for compliance substantiation:*

- **Policy / Procedure Documents** — Approved policies, SOPs, or guidelines implementing directive requirements
- **Technical Configuration Records** — System configurations, screenshots, hardening records
- **Audit Reports** — Internal or external audit findings confirming compliance
- **Test Results / Assessment Reports** — Penetration test reports, vulnerability assessment results
- **Training Records** — Attendance records, completion certificates for mandated training
- **Incident Reports** — Documented incidents and responses demonstrating compliance with notification obligations
- **Meeting Minutes** — Board or committee minutes approving compliance actions
- **Vendor Certifications** — Third-party compliance certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2, etc.)
- **System Logs** — Automated logs evidencing controls are operational
- **Correspondence** — Formal communications with NACSA (submissions, acknowledgements)

## 11. Appendices

### Appendix A — Directive Receipt and Logging Procedure

*This appendix provides step-by-step guidance for registering a newly received NACSA directive.*

1. Upon receipt of a directive from NACSA (by any channel), the receiving officer must immediately notify the Compliance Officer.
2. The Compliance Officer assigns an Internal Reference Number using the format `[ORG]-DIR-[YYYY]-[NNN]`.
3. The original directive document is stored in the designated evidence repository under the assigned reference.
4. An acknowledgement of receipt is sent to NACSA within the timeframe stipulated in the directive or, where not specified, within **[X] business days**.
5. A new entry is created in this register under Sections 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, and 7.
6. The CISO and relevant Business Unit owners are notified of the directive's requirements.
7. A compliance review meeting is scheduled within **[X] business days** of receipt.
