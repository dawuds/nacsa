## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This register maintains a complete and current inventory of all licensed cyber security service providers engaged by [Organization Name] in accordance with the **Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854)**, administered by the **National Cyber Security Agency (NACSA)**. It serves as the primary reference document to demonstrate regulatory compliance and to support procurement, audit, and incident response activities.

This document addresses the following statutory obligations:

- **Section 28, Act 854** — Requirement to engage only NACSA-licensed entities for prescribed cyber security services.
- **Section 32, Act 854** — Obligations of the engaging organization regarding verification, record-keeping, and notification in relation to licensed service providers.

### 1.2 Scope

This register applies to:

- All third-party and contracted cyber security service providers engaged by [Organization Name], whether engaged directly or through an intermediary.
- All service classes regulated under Act 854 for which a NACSA licence is a statutory prerequisite.
- All business units, subsidiaries, and branches of [Organization Name] that procure or consume cyber security services from external providers.

This register **excludes** internal employees performing cyber security functions as part of their employment duties, unless those functions are provided under a separate entity arrangement requiring licensure.

## 5. Licence Verification and Monitoring

*This section documents the process by which [Organization Name] verifies and monitors the ongoing validity of NACSA licences for all engaged service providers. Verification records must be retained as evidence of due diligence under s32 of Act 854.*

### 5.1 Verification Schedule

| Trigger | Action Required | Responsible Party | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| New engagement onboarding | Verify licence on NACSA portal; obtain copy of licence certificate | Procurement / CISO Office | Before contract execution |
| Quarterly register review | Verify all active licences remain valid via NACSA portal | CISO Office | Within first 10 business days of each quarter |
| 90 days before licence expiry | Issue expiry alert to contract owner and provider; confirm renewal status | CISO Office / Procurement | 90 days prior to expiry |
| 30 days before licence expiry | Escalate to CISO; assess continuity risk; initiate contingency planning | CISO | 30 days prior to expiry |
| Licence lapse or suspension | Suspend engagement; notify NACSA if required; activate contingency plan | CISO | Immediately upon discovery |
| Contract renewal | Re-verify licence validity at point of renewal | Procurement | Prior to contract renewal execution |

### 6.2 Record Retention

- Active engagement records: Maintained throughout the engagement and for **7 years** following engagement termination.
- Licence verification logs: Retained for **7 years**.
- Expired/terminated entries: Archived in Section 3.2; not deleted.
- Retention basis: Section 32, Act 854; [Organization Name] Records Management Policy [Policy Reference].

### 6.3 Escalation Procedure

| Scenario | Escalation Path | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Licence expiry not renewed within 30 days of expiry | CISO → Board Risk Committee | Within 2 business days of discovery |
| NACSA suspends or revokes a provider's licence mid-engagement | CISO → Legal → Board; notify NACSA if required under s32 | Immediately |
| Provider unable to demonstrate valid licence on request | Suspend engagement; CISO to investigate | Within 1 business day |
| Potential unlicensed service provision discovered retrospectively | Legal review; CISO to notify NACSA; document remediation | Within 5 business days |

## 7. Review and Approval

### 7.1 Review Cycle

This register shall be reviewed on a **quarterly** basis, or immediately upon any of the following triggers:

- Onboarding of a new cyber security service provider.
- Termination or expiry of an existing engagement.
- NACSA notification of licence suspension, revocation, or renewal for any registered provider.
- Material change in the scope of services delivered under any engagement.
- Direction from NACSA, BNM, or internal audit.
