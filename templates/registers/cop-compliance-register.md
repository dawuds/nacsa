## 1. Purpose and Scope

This CoP Compliance Register is a living compliance management instrument maintained by [Organization Name] to systematically track and evidence its adherence to the sector-specific Code of Practice (CoP) issued pursuant to **Section 24 of the Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854)**. The register records each discrete CoP requirement, its current compliance status, supporting evidence, identified gaps, and associated remediation activities.

**Scope:** This register applies to all systems, processes, personnel, and third-party arrangements within [Organization Name] that fall under the designated critical information infrastructure (CII) sector as determined by the National Cyber Security Agency (NACSA). It encompasses all CoP requirements applicable to the organization's sector designation and shall be maintained as the primary audit artifact for demonstrating ongoing compliance with Act 854.

## 10. Escalation Matrix

*The following thresholds govern mandatory escalation of compliance issues identified through this register.*

| Scenario | Escalation Trigger | Escalate To | Timeframe | Action Required |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Critical gap identified | Any CoP requirement classified as Critical non-compliance | CISO and Risk Committee | Within 5 business days of identification | Immediate remediation plan; interim controls implemented |
| High gap unresolved past target date | High-classification gap overdue by more than 30 days | CISO | Within 5 business days of becoming overdue | Revised remediation plan with board visibility |
| Multiple High/Critical gaps open simultaneously | 3 or more High or Critical gaps open at same time | Risk Committee | At next scheduled committee meeting; emergency meeting if imminent regulatory exposure | Board-level risk acceptance or emergency remediation programme |
| NACSA enquiry or audit notification | Receipt of NACSA audit notice or regulatory enquiry | Board / Senior Management | Immediately upon receipt | Activate regulatory response protocol; brief Legal & Regulatory Affairs |
| Overall compliance score below threshold | Register score falls below [70%] | Risk Committee | At next quarterly review | Root cause analysis; enhanced monitoring; remediation programme |

## 13. Appendices

### Appendix A — Compliance Status Definitions and Assessment Criteria

*This appendix provides detailed criteria for each compliance status classification to ensure consistent assessment across reviewers and review cycles.*

| Status | Definition | Assessment Criteria | Evidence Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Compliant** | The organization fully meets the CoP requirement | All sub-requirements addressed; controls implemented and operating effectively; evidence is current and sufficient | Full evidence package; no material gaps |
| **Partially Compliant** | The organization substantially meets the requirement but identified gaps remain | Core requirement met; minor or procedural gaps identified; remediation in progress | Partial evidence package; gap and remediation plan documented |
| **Non-Compliant** | The organization does not meet the requirement | Material gaps exist; controls absent, ineffective, or not implemented | Mandatory: gap description and approved remediation plan |
| **Not Applicable** | The requirement does not apply to this organization | Written justification approved by Compliance Officer; justification filed in Section 6 | Justification document; Compliance Officer approval |
| **Under Review** | Compliance status is being assessed | Evidence collection or assessment in progress; interim status | Review completion date recorded; interim status valid for no more than 30 days |

### Appendix C — Register Maintenance Procedure

*This appendix outlines the operational procedure for maintaining this register.*

**Quarterly Review Procedure:**

1. Compliance Officer initiates review no later than [14] days before quarter end.
2. Evidence owners (System/Process Owners) submit updated evidence for each assigned CoP requirement.
3. Compliance Officer reviews evidence, updates compliance status, and documents any new gaps.
4. All overdue remediation items are escalated per the escalation matrix in Section 10.
5. Updated register is reviewed by CISO.
6. Register summary is presented to the Risk Committee.
7. Approved register version is archived in [Document Management System].
8. Next review date is confirmed and recorded.

**Trigger-Based Updates (outside quarterly cycle):**

- New CoP version issued by NACSA — register must be updated within [30] days.
- Material cyber security incident — relevant register entries reviewed within [10] business days.
- Significant system or process change — affected register entries reviewed within [20] business days.
- NACSA audit notification — full register review completed within [5] business days.
