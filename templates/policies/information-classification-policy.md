## 1. Purpose

*Describe the intent of this policy and its importance to the organisation's information security posture and regulatory compliance obligations.*

This policy establishes a formal framework for classifying information processed, stored, or transmitted by [Organization Name] in its capacity as a National Critical Information Infrastructure (NCII) entity, as designated under the Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854). The policy defines classification levels, associated handling requirements, labelling standards, declassification procedures, and restrictions on information sharing in accordance with Section 18 and Section 49 of Act 854.

The purpose of this policy is to:

- Ensure that information assets are appropriately protected commensurate with their sensitivity, value, and criticality to [Organization Name] and to national cyber security;
- Provide clear and consistent guidance to all personnel on how to handle, store, transmit, label, and share information;
- Fulfil obligations as an NCII entity under Act 854, including mandatory reporting and information governance obligations imposed by NACSA;
- Reduce the risk of unauthorised disclosure, accidental loss, or misuse of sensitive information;
- Support a culture of information security awareness and accountability throughout [Organization Name].

## 5. Information Classification Levels

*Define the classification taxonomy used by [Organization Name]. The four-tier model below is recommended. Adapt descriptions to reflect the organisation's specific context and NCII sector.*

[Organization Name] adopts a four-tier information classification framework applicable to all information assets within scope. Classification shall be assigned at the point of creation or receipt and shall reflect the most sensitive element contained within the information asset.

### 5.1 Classification Taxonomy Overview

| Classification Level | Label | Description | Example Information Types |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Confidential** | CONFIDENTIAL | The highest classification level. Information whose unauthorised disclosure would cause serious harm to [Organization Name], customers, national security, or critical national infrastructure. Restricted to specifically authorised individuals on a strict need-to-know basis. | NCII system architecture diagrams, incident response reports submitted to NACSA, cryptographic keys, customer PII (sensitive categories), board deliberations, M&A documentation |
| **Restricted** | RESTRICTED | Information whose unauthorised disclosure would cause significant harm or reputational damage. Access limited to employees and authorised third parties with a demonstrated business need. | Internal audit reports, vulnerability assessment results, employee HR records, contract terms, financial forecasts, non-public regulatory correspondence |
| **Internal** | INTERNAL | Information intended for internal use within [Organization Name]. Disclosure to external parties without authorisation is not permitted but would not cause serious harm. | Internal policies and procedures, meeting minutes, general operational data, internal announcements |
| **Public** | PUBLIC | Information approved for unrestricted public disclosure. No handling restrictions apply beyond accuracy and integrity checks prior to release. | Marketing materials, published annual reports, publicly available regulatory filings, press releases |

### 5.2 Classification Assignment Principles

*Describe the principles that govern how information is classified.*

When determining the appropriate classification level, Information Owners shall apply the following principles:

- **Highest Element Principle:** A document or dataset shall be classified at the level of its most sensitive component. A report containing one Confidential section shall be classified Confidential in its entirety.
- **Need-to-Know:** Classification shall reflect the minimum level necessary to protect the information, not the maximum that could theoretically apply.
- **Default Classification:** Where an Information Owner is uncertain of the appropriate classification, information shall default to **Restricted** pending formal review.
- **Aggregation Risk:** Collections of lower-classified information may warrant a higher classification when combined. Information Owners must consider aggregation risk when classifying datasets or composite documents.
- **Dynamic Review:** Classification shall be reviewed whenever the sensitivity of the underlying information materially changes, upon a change in regulatory status, or upon scheduled review.

### 5.3 Classification Decision Guide

*Provide a practical decision aid for Information Owners.*

Information Owners should ask the following questions to determine the appropriate classification:

1. **If this information were disclosed without authorisation, what is the worst reasonably foreseeable impact?**
   - Catastrophic impact to [Organization Name], customers, or national security → **Confidential**
   - Significant harm or reputational damage → **Restricted**
   - Minor inconvenience or limited impact → **Internal**
   - No harm; information is intended for public consumption → **Public**

2. **Is this information subject to a specific regulatory protection obligation?**
   - Yes — Act 854, s49 disclosure restrictions, PDPA sensitive personal data, BNM RMiT → **Confidential** or **Restricted** as appropriate
   - No specific obligation → apply general principles above

3. **Is this information shared with or received from NACSA or another government agency under Act 854?**
   - Yes → **Confidential** minimum, subject to s49 restrictions (see Section 9)

## 6. Handling Requirements Per Classification Level

*Define specific, actionable handling requirements for each classification level across the key handling domains: storage, transmission, access control, printing, disposal, and third-party sharing.*

### 6.1 Confidential

| Handling Domain | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Storage — Physical** | Locked cabinets or safes; access restricted to authorised individuals; visitor access prohibited to storage areas |
| **Storage — Digital** | Encrypted at rest using AES-256 or equivalent; access controlled via role-based access control (RBAC) and multi-factor authentication (MFA); stored only on approved [Organization Name] systems |
| **Transmission — Internal** | Encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2 minimum); transmitted only via approved secure channels; no transmission via personal email or unapproved messaging applications |
| **Transmission — External** | End-to-end encryption mandatory; prior written approval from CISO or delegated authority; secure file transfer protocols only (e.g., SFTP, encrypted email with certificate); logging of all external transmissions |
| **Access Control** | Strict need-to-know; individually named access list maintained by Information Owner; MFA required; access reviewed quarterly |
| **Printing** | Print only when operationally necessary; collect immediately from printer; printers must be in secured areas; no unattended printing |
| **Labelling** | "CONFIDENTIAL — [Organization Name]" header and footer on all pages; electronic files labelled in filename and document properties |
| **Disposal — Physical** | Cross-cut shredding (DIN 66399 security level P-4 minimum) or certified destruction with destruction certificate |
| **Disposal — Digital** | Secure overwrite (NIST SP 800-88 compliant) or cryptographic erasure; certificate of destruction retained |
| **Third-Party Sharing** | Requires CISO approval and signed Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA); subject to s49 Act 854 restrictions (see Section 9); minimum disclosure principle applies |
| **Incident Reporting** | Any suspected unauthorised access or disclosure must be reported to the CISO within 1 hour of discovery |

### 6.2 Restricted

| Handling Domain | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Storage — Physical** | Locked drawers or cabinets; access limited to relevant team members |
| **Storage — Digital** | Encrypted at rest; access controlled via RBAC; stored only on approved [Organization Name] systems |
| **Transmission — Internal** | Approved [Organization Name] communication channels; no transmission via personal accounts |
| **Transmission — External** | Encryption required; Line Manager approval; NDA in place with recipient organisation |
| **Access Control** | Need-to-know within relevant business unit; MFA recommended; access reviewed semi-annually |
| **Printing** | Collect promptly from printer; not to be left unattended in shared areas |
| **Labelling** | "RESTRICTED — [Organization Name]" header and footer on all pages |
| **Disposal — Physical** | Cross-cut shredding or secure disposal bin |
| **Disposal — Digital** | Secure delete; standard disposal process for approved equipment |
| **Third-Party Sharing** | Requires Line Manager approval and NDA; minimum disclosure principle applies |
| **Incident Reporting** | Suspected unauthorised disclosure reported to Line Manager and CISO within 4 hours |

### 6.3 Internal

| Handling Domain | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Storage — Physical** | Standard office controls; not to be left accessible to visitors |
| **Storage — Digital** | Standard access controls on [Organization Name] systems; no storage on personal devices without approval |
| **Transmission — Internal** | Standard [Organization Name] communication tools |
| **Transmission — External** | Prior approval from Line Manager; must not be sent to personal email accounts |
| **Access Control** | All employees; contractors with active agreements; no broad public access |
| **Printing** | Standard printing; retrieve promptly |

| **Disposal — Physical** | Shredding recommended; secure disposal bins |
| **Disposal — Digital** | Standard delete acceptable for end-of-life documents |

### 6.4 Public

## 8. Declassification Procedures

*Define the formal process for reducing or removing the classification level of information assets, including approval requirements, triggers, and documentation.*

### 8.1 Principles

Declassification shall only occur when:

- The sensitivity of the information has genuinely and permanently reduced;
- The regulatory or business obligation that necessitated the original classification no longer applies;
- A formal review has confirmed that declassification does not introduce unacceptable risk;
- Appropriate approvals have been obtained.

Declassification does not occur automatically by the passage of time. A formal decision must be made and documented.

### 8.2 Triggers for Declassification Review

*List circumstances that should prompt an Information Owner to review and potentially declassify an information asset.*

- Expiry of a confidentiality period specified at the time of classification;
- Resolution of a cyber security incident to which the information relates;
- Completion of a transaction, project, or regulatory process;
- Change in the regulatory status of the information;
- Scheduled periodic review of classification levels (minimum annual);
- Request from a senior stakeholder or regulator.

### 8.3 Declassification Approval Authority

| Proposed Declassification | Required Approver |
|---|---|
| Confidential → Restricted | CISO + Information Owner |
| Confidential → Internal | CISO + Information Owner + [Senior Management Role, e.g., CRO] |
| Confidential → Public | CISO + Information Owner + [Board-level or equivalent approval] |
| Restricted → Internal | Information Owner + Line Manager |
| Restricted → Public | Information Owner + CISO |
| Internal → Public | Information Owner + [Communications/Legal] |

### 8.4 Declassification Procedure

*Provide step-by-step instructions for the declassification process.*

1. **Initiation:** The Information Owner, or a delegated representative, submits a Declassification Request Form (see Appendix B) to the CISO or designated approver.

2. **Assessment:** The approving authority reviews:
   - The current classification and the basis on which it was originally assigned;
   - The proposed new classification and justification;
   - Any regulatory restrictions that may prevent declassification (particularly s49 Act 854 obligations);
   - The potential impact of declassification on [Organization Name], customers, or third parties.

3. **Approval or Rejection:**
   - If approved: The Information Owner updates the classification label on all copies of the information asset and records the change in the Information Asset Register.
   - If rejected: The Information Owner is notified with reasons. Classification remains unchanged. The decision is documented.

4. **Notification:** Relevant Information Custodians and Users are notified of the reclassification within **[X business days]** of approval.

5. **Record Retention:** All declassification requests, supporting assessments, and decisions shall be retained for a minimum of **[X years]** in accordance with [Organization Name]'s Records Management Policy.

### 8.5 Reclassification (Upward)

Information may be reclassified to a higher level at any time by the Information Owner if the sensitivity of the information is found to have increased. Upward reclassification does not require the same level of approval as declassification but must be documented and communicated to all Information Custodians and Users who hold copies of the information.

## 9. Information Sharing Restrictions

*Define the controls and procedures governing the sharing of classified information, with particular reference to restrictions imposed by Section 49 of the Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854).*

### 9.1 General Principles

Information sharing shall be governed by the following principles:

- **Minimum Disclosure:** Share only the information necessary to fulfil a specific, legitimate business or regulatory purpose.
- **Need-to-Know:** Recipients must have a genuine need for the information to perform their authorised functions.
- **Authorised Channels:** Information shall only be shared via approved, secure channels commensurate with the classification level (see Section 6).
- **Documented Agreements:** Sharing with external parties must be underpinned by appropriate legal agreements (NDA, Data Sharing Agreement, contract clauses).
- **Regulatory Priority:** Sharing obligations imposed by law or a competent regulatory authority (including NACSA) take precedence over internal restrictions, subject to legal advice.

### 9.2 Restrictions Under Section 49, Cyber Security Act 2024

*This section implements the disclosure restrictions imposed by s49 Act 854. Legal counsel should review this section to ensure accuracy in the context of [Organization Name]'s specific regulatory position.*

Section 49 of the Cyber Security Act 2024 restricts the disclosure of information obtained by persons in the exercise of duties or powers under Act 854. The following controls apply to all information received from, shared with, or generated in connection with NACSA or in the exercise of NCII obligations:

| Restriction | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Prohibition on Unauthorised Disclosure** | No employee, contractor, or agent of [Organization Name] shall disclose information obtained in the exercise of duties under Act 854 except as permitted by Act 854 or authorised by the Director General of NACSA or other competent authority. |
| **Internal Access Controls** | Access to information subject to s49 restrictions shall be limited to a specifically named access list approved by the CISO. |
| **No Onward Sharing Without Authorisation** | Information received from NACSA or generated in connection with Act 854 compliance shall not be shared with any third party, including subsidiaries, without the prior written authorisation of NACSA or as otherwise permitted by law. |
| **Legal and Regulatory Exceptions** | Sharing is permitted where required by a court order, other statutory obligation, or written authorisation from the Director General of NACSA. All such sharing events must be documented and reported to the CISO. |
| **Breach Consequences** | Unauthorised disclosure of information subject to s49 restrictions may constitute an offence under Act 854 and expose [Organization Name] and the responsible individual to criminal penalties. |

### 9.3 Information Sharing with Third Parties

*Define the process for sharing classified information with external parties, including vendors, regulators, auditors, and international counterparts.*

| Recipient Category | Maximum Classification Shareable | Approval Required | Agreement Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NACSA / Government Agencies** | Confidential | CISO notification | Regulatory requirement; NDA not required |
| **External Auditors** | Restricted (Confidential with CISO approval) | CISO approval for Confidential | NDA + Audit Engagement Letter |
| **Vendors / Managed Service Providers** | Restricted | CISO approval | NDA + Data Processing Agreement |
| **Legal Counsel (External)** | Confidential | CISO awareness | Legal Professional Privilege; engagement letter |
| **International Partners** | Restricted (Confidential with CISO + [CEO/Board] approval) | CISO + senior approval | NDA + cross-border data transfer controls |
| **Media / Public** | Public only | Communications team approval | None |

### 9.4 Information Sharing Register

[Organization Name] shall maintain an Information Sharing Register recording all instances of external sharing of Confidential and Restricted information. The register shall capture:

- Date of sharing;
- Information shared (reference, not full content);
- Classification level;
- Recipient organisation and named contact;
- Legal basis or approval authority;
- Expiry or review date of sharing arrangement;
- Agreement reference (NDA, DPA).

*The Information Sharing Register shall be maintained by [Designated Role] and reviewed by the CISO quarterly.*

## 11. Non-Compliance and Enforcement

*Define the consequences of non-compliance and the escalation and enforcement process.*

### 11.1 Non-Compliance Indicators

Non-compliance with this policy includes, but is not limited to:

- Failing to classify or label information assets;
- Handling, storing, or transmitting information in a manner inconsistent with its classification level;
- Sharing classified information with unauthorised recipients;
- Failing to report a suspected classification-related incident;
- Removing or altering classification labels without authorisation;
- Deliberate or reckless disclosure of information subject to s49 Act 854 restrictions.

### 11.2 Consequences of Non-Compliance

| Severity | Example Incident | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| **Minor** | Failure to label a single Internal document | Informal counselling; mandatory refresher training |
| **Moderate** | Sharing Restricted information without Line Manager approval | Formal written warning; mandatory retraining; access review |
| **Serious** | Unauthorised external disclosure of Confidential information | Formal disciplinary action up to and including termination; notification to CISO; regulatory notification considered |
| **Critical** | Deliberate disclosure of s49-restricted information | Immediate suspension; escalation to [Organization Name] Board; referral to NACSA; potential criminal prosecution under Act 854 |

### 11.3 Reporting Non-Compliance

All employees must report suspected or actual non-compliance with this policy immediately to their Line Manager and to the CISO via [Incident Reporting Channel]. Reports may be made anonymously via [Whistleblowing Channel] where [Organization Name]'s Whistleblowing Policy permits.

### 11.4 Policy Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy may only be granted by the CISO in writing. All exceptions must:

- State the specific requirement from which an exception is sought;
- Document the business justification;
- Include a compensating control to mitigate the associated risk;
- Be time-limited with a defined review date;
- Be recorded in the Policy Exception Register maintained by [Designated Role].
