## 1. Purpose

*State the primary objectives of this policy. Explain why the policy exists and what it is intended to achieve within the organization's cyber security governance framework.*

This policy establishes [Organization Name]'s formal approach to cyber security auditing and regulatory compliance as required under the **Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854)**, administered by the **National Cyber Security Agency (NACSA)**. It defines the obligations, processes, and accountability structures necessary to ensure that the organization meets its statutory duties as a **Critical Entity (CE)** or operator of **Critical Information Infrastructure (CII)** under the Act.

The specific objectives of this policy are to:

- Define the frequency, scope, and methodology of cyber security audits conducted by or on behalf of [Organization Name].
- Establish requirements for auditor independence and professional competency.
- Provide a structured framework for classifying audit findings and managing remediation activities.
- Ensure continuous monitoring of the organization's posture against applicable regulatory obligations under Act 854.
- Implement a systematic process for tracking and responding to directives issued by NACSA to Critical Entities.
- Define the organization's approach to assessing compliance against the applicable **Sector Code of Practice (CoP)** designated under Act 854.

## 5. Policy Principles

*State the high-level principles that underpin this policy. These should reflect the organization's values and risk appetite in relation to cyber security audit and compliance.*

[Organization Name] adopts the following principles in its approach to cyber security audit and compliance:

**5.1 Accountability**
The Board of Directors and Senior Management are ultimately accountable for ensuring the organization meets its obligations as a Critical Entity under Act 854. Audit findings and compliance status shall be reported to the Board Risk Committee (or equivalent) at least annually.

**5.2 Independence**
Cyber security audits shall be conducted by parties who are independent of the systems, functions, and processes being audited. Independence requirements are detailed in Section 7 of this policy.

**5.3 Risk-Based Approach**
The scope and intensity of audit activities shall be proportionate to the organization's risk profile, the criticality of systems under review, and the findings of prior audit cycles.

**5.4 Transparency and Timely Reporting**
Audit findings and compliance deficiencies shall be reported to relevant stakeholders, including NACSA where required, in a timely and accurate manner. There shall be no suppression, delay, or misrepresentation of audit outcomes.

**5.5 Continuous Improvement**
The cyber security audit and compliance programme shall be treated as an instrument of continuous improvement. Remediation of findings shall be tracked to closure, and lessons learned shall inform future audit scopes and security investments.

**5.6 Regulatory Cooperation**
[Organization Name] is committed to full cooperation with NACSA and other competent authorities in the exercise of their powers under Act 854, including inspections, investigations, and responses to directives.

## 7. Auditor Independence Requirements

*Establish requirements for the independence, qualifications, and objectivity of cyber security auditors. This section supports the integrity of the audit process and aligns with Section 22 of Act 854.*

### 7.1 Independence Principles

To ensure audit findings are objective and credible, auditors conducting cyber security audits on behalf of [Organization Name] shall satisfy the following independence requirements:

**7.1.1 Organizational Independence**
- Internal auditors assigned to cyber security audits shall report functionally to the Audit Committee of the Board, not to operational management responsible for CII.
- Internal auditors shall not audit functions, systems, or processes for which they have had operational responsibility within the preceding **[24] months**.
- External auditors shall have no material financial or business relationship with [Organization Name] beyond the audit engagement.

**7.1.2 Independence Declarations**
All auditors, both internal and external, shall complete and sign an **Independence Declaration** prior to commencement of each audit engagement. Declarations shall be retained as part of the audit working papers.

| Declaration Element | Requirement |

| Personal relationships with audited personnel | Must be disclosed and assessed |
| Prior engagements at [Organization Name] | Disclosed in declaration |

## 8. Finding Classification and Remediation

*Establish a standardized system for classifying audit findings by severity and define the remediation process, timelines, and escalation procedures.*

### 8.1 Finding Classification Framework

All findings identified during cyber security audits shall be classified using the following severity framework:

| Severity | Label | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Critical** | Critical | A finding that represents an immediate, material threat to CII availability, integrity, or confidentiality; or constitutes a breach of a mandatory statutory obligation under Act 854. | Unpatched critical vulnerability on CII, absence of required statutory reporting, active compromise indicators. |
| **2 — High** | High | A significant deficiency in a key control that, if exploited or unaddressed, is likely to result in material harm or regulatory non-compliance within the near term. | Inadequate privileged access controls, absence of network segmentation, non-compliant audit log retention. |
| **3 — Medium** | Medium | A control gap or deficiency that poses moderate risk and requires remediation within a defined period. | Incomplete user access reviews, gaps in third-party security assessments, outdated incident response procedures. |
| **4 — Low** | Low | A minor deficiency or improvement opportunity with limited immediate risk, but which should be addressed to strengthen the overall control environment. | Minor documentation gaps, process inefficiencies, low-risk configuration deviations. |
| **Observation** | Observation | A positive observation, best practice recommendation, or advisory note not representing a control deficiency. | Enhancement opportunities, emerging practice recommendations. |

### 8.2 Mandatory Remediation Timelines

| Severity | Initial Management Response | Remediation Completion | Escalation if Overdue |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | 2 business days | 15 calendar days | Immediate — CISO and CEO |
| **High** | 5 business days | 45 calendar days | Head of Internal Audit and CISO |
| **Medium** | 10 business days | 90 calendar days | Head of Internal Audit |
| **Low** | 30 calendar days | 180 calendar days | Internal Audit |

*Timelines may be extended with documented justification approved by the CISO (for High/Medium/Low) or the CEO (for Critical). NACSA must be notified of any timeline extension for findings related to statutory obligations.*

### 8.3 Remediation Planning

For each finding rated **High** or above, the responsible business unit shall prepare a **Remediation Plan** within the initial response deadline, containing:

- Detailed description of the finding and root cause analysis.
- Proposed corrective action(s).
- Interim compensating controls (if applicable).
- Remediation owner (name and role).
- Target completion date.
- Evidence of completion (what will be provided to Internal Audit for closure verification).

| Remediation Plan Element | Required for Critical | Required for High | Required for Medium | Required for Low |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Root Cause Analysis | Yes | Yes | Recommended | No |
| Interim Compensating Controls | Yes | Yes | If applicable | No |
| Board/Senior Management notification | Yes | Yes | No | No |
| NACSA notification | As directed | As directed | No | No |

### 8.4 Finding Closure and Verification

*Internal Audit shall verify the satisfactory completion of all remediation actions before formally closing a finding.*

- Closure verification shall be conducted by Internal Audit through evidence review and, where appropriate, re-testing.
- Findings shall not be closed based solely on management assertion without supporting evidence.
- Partial closures shall not be accepted for Critical findings; all agreed actions must be completed prior to closure.
- Recurring findings (same finding identified in two or more consecutive audit cycles) shall be escalated to the Audit Committee with a documented root cause analysis.

### 8.5 Finding Register

*[Organization Name] shall maintain a centralized Audit Finding Register capturing all findings and their remediation status.*

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Finding ID | Unique identifier |
| Audit Engagement Reference | Link to parent audit |
| Date Identified | Date finding was raised |
| Severity | Critical / High / Medium / Low / Observation |
| Finding Description | Detailed description |
| Affected System/Process | CII or process reference |
| Root Cause | Identified root cause |
| Remediation Owner | Name and designation |
| Agreed Remediation Actions | Description of corrective actions |

## 10. Critical Entity Directive Tracking

*Establish the process for receiving, acknowledging, actioning, and reporting on directives issued by NACSA to [Organization Name] as a Critical Entity under Section 23 of Act 854.*

### 10.1 Directive Receipt and Registration

Upon receipt of any directive from NACSA issued pursuant to Act 854, the following steps shall be taken within **2 business days**:

1. The directive shall be formally acknowledged to NACSA by the CISO or authorized delegate.
2. The directive shall be registered in the **CE Directive Register** (see Section 10.3).
3. The CISO shall notify the CEO and relevant Senior Management of the directive and its required actions.
4. A designated **Directive Response Owner** shall be assigned for each directive.

### 10.2 Directive Response Process

| Step | Activity | Responsible Party | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Receipt and acknowledgement | CISO | 2 business days of receipt |
| 2 | Register directive in CE Directive Register | CISO / Compliance Team | 2 business days of receipt |
| 3 | Assess impact and required actions | CISO and Directive Response Owner | 5 business days of receipt |
| 4 | Develop response / action plan | Directive Response Owner | As required by directive |
| 5 | Senior Management approval of response plan | CEO / Senior Management | Prior to submission to NACSA |
| 6 | Submit response to NACSA (if required) | CISO | Within NACSA-prescribed timeframe |
| 7 | Execute required actions | Directive Response Owner | Per agreed action plan |
| 8 | Report progress to NACSA | CISO | Per NACSA requirements |
| 9 | Close directive upon NACSA confirmation | Compliance Team | Upon NACSA confirmation |
| 10 | Retain records | Compliance Team | Per records retention policy |

### 10.3 CE Directive Register

*[Organization Name] shall maintain a CE Directive Register as the authoritative record of all directives received from NACSA.*

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Directive Reference | NACSA-assigned reference number |
| Date Received | Date directive was received |
| Date Acknowledged | Date acknowledgement sent to NACSA |
| Directive Summary | Brief description of the directive |
| Statutory Basis | Act 854 section under which directive was issued |
| Response Deadline | Deadline prescribed by NACSA |
| Required Actions | Summary of actions required |
| Directive Response Owner | Name and designation |
| Status | Open / In Progress / Submitted / Closed |
| Response Submission Date | Date response submitted to NACSA |
| Closure Date | Date directive formally closed |
| Supporting Documents | Reference to response documents |

### 10.4 Escalation for Non-Compliance with Directives

Failure to comply with a NACSA directive within the prescribed timeframe constitutes a significant regulatory risk. The following escalation protocol shall apply:

- **5 business days before deadline:** CISO to confirm all actions are on track; escalate to CEO if any risk of non-compliance.
- **Immediately upon identification of risk of non-compliance:** CISO to brief CEO and Board Audit Committee; consider proactive communication to NACSA.
- **Non-compliance:** CEO to lead management response; engage external legal counsel if required; document all mitigating actions taken.

### 13.2 Escalation Thresholds

The following conditions shall trigger immediate escalation to Senior Management and/or the Board:

- Identification of a **Critical** audit finding.
- Receipt of a NACSA directive with a compliance deadline of less than **[15] calendar days**.
- Risk of non-compliance with a NACSA directive.
- Identification of a recurring finding (same finding in two or more consecutive audits).
- Discovery of a potential or confirmed breach of Act 854.
- Material change in the organization's CII designation status.
- External auditor qualified opinion or adverse finding in the audit report.

## 14. Non-Compliance and Exceptions

### 14.1 Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with the requirements of this policy may result in:

- Disciplinary action in accordance with [Organization Name]'s Human Resources policies.
- Escalation to the Board Risk Committee and/or Board of Directors.
- Notification to NACSA where non-compliance constitutes a breach of Act 854.
- Engagement of external legal counsel and regulatory remediation activities.

Potential breaches of this policy shall be reported to the Head of Internal Audit and CISO within **[2] business days** of discovery.

### 14.2 Exceptions

*Define the process for requesting and approving exceptions to this policy.*

Exceptions to any requirement of this policy may only be granted in exceptional circumstances and must be:

1. Submitted in writing to the CISO using the **Policy Exception Request Form** ([Appendix D]).
2. Accompanied by a documented business justification and risk assessment.
3. Reviewed and approved by the CISO and Head of Internal Audit.
4. Escalated to the Board Risk Committee for exceptions affecting statutory obligations under Act 854.
5. Time-bound, with a defined expiry date and compensating controls.
6. Recorded in the **Policy Exception Register** maintained by the Compliance team.

| Exception Element | Requirement |

| Maximum exception period | [12] months |
| Renewal process | Full re-submission and re-approval required |
| Exceptions involving NACSA obligations | Must be flagged to Legal for advice |
