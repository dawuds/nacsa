## 1. Purpose

*Explain the intent of this policy and the regulatory obligation it fulfils. Reference the specific Act and section.*

This Acceptable Use Policy ("Policy") establishes the standards and obligations governing the acceptable use of National Critical Information Infrastructure (NCII) systems, networks, data, and related technology assets owned or operated by [Organization Name] ("the Organisation").

This Policy is issued in compliance with **Section 18 of the Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854)**, which mandates that NCII entities implement and enforce policies governing the acceptable use of NCII assets to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of critical information infrastructure within Malaysia's financial services sector.

The objectives of this Policy are to:

- Define the boundaries of authorised and prohibited use of the Organisation's NCII systems and assets.
- Protect the Organisation's information assets, reputation, and operational continuity.
- Establish clear expectations for all personnel regarding their obligations under Malaysian law, including the Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854), the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA), and Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) regulatory instruments.
- Provide a basis for monitoring, investigation, and enforcement of acceptable use standards.

## 5. Authorised Use of NCII Systems

*Detail the conditions under which personnel are permitted to use Organisation NCII systems. This section sets the positive grant of access and the conditions attached to it.*

### 5.1 Basis of Authorised Access

Access to NCII systems is a **privilege**, not a right. All authorised access must be:

- Formally provisioned through the Organisation's **Access Management Policy and Procedure**.
- Limited to the minimum access required to perform legitimate job functions (principle of least privilege).
- Approved by the relevant system owner and the personnel's direct line manager.
- Reviewed at minimum on a **[quarterly/semi-annual]** basis.

### 5.2 Conditions of Authorised Use

Personnel granted access to NCII systems are authorised to use such systems solely for:

- **Business purposes** directly related to their assigned roles and responsibilities within [Organization Name].
- **Approved personal incidental use** as specified in Section 5.3 below, where such use does not interfere with business operations or consume material Organisation resources.
- **Authorised security testing** and system administration activities, where formally scoped and approved in writing by the CISO.
- **Training and development** activities on Organisation-approved learning platforms.

### 5.3 Incidental Personal Use

*Define the extent of permissible personal use, if any. Some organisations prohibit all personal use; others allow limited incidental use. Align this with your HR policies.*

[Organization Name] **[permits/does not permit]** limited incidental personal use of NCII systems subject to the following conditions:

- [ ] Personal use must not involve access to or transmission of inappropriate, offensive, or unlawful content.
- [ ] Personal use must not materially impact system performance or network bandwidth.
- [ ] Personal use must not conflict with any obligation under this Policy or other Organisation policies.
- [ ] Personnel acknowledge that personal use on Organisation systems remains subject to monitoring as described in Section 10.

> *[If personal use is not permitted, replace the above with: "Personal use of NCII systems is strictly prohibited. All use of Organisation systems must be for legitimate business purposes only."]*

### 5.4 Access Credentials

All personnel must:

| Obligation | Detail |
|---|---|
| Protect credentials | Never share usernames, passwords, PINs, or authentication tokens with any other person, including IT staff |
| Use assigned credentials only | Log in using only their own assigned credentials |
| Report compromise immediately | Report any suspected or confirmed compromise of credentials to the IT Security team within **[X hours]** of discovery |
| Comply with password policy | Create and maintain passwords in compliance with the Organisation's **Password Management Policy** |
| Use MFA | Comply with mandatory multi-factor authentication (MFA) requirements for all system access |

## 6. Prohibited Activities

*This is a critical section. Be explicit and comprehensive. List all prohibited activities to ensure personnel have clear notice and to support enforcement action.*

### 6.1 Absolute Prohibitions

The following activities are **strictly prohibited** on NCII systems under all circumstances. Violations may result in immediate suspension of access, disciplinary action, and referral to law enforcement authorities:

| # | Prohibited Activity | Relevant Law |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Unauthorised access to any system, account, or data | Act 854 s18; Computer Crimes Act 1997 |
| 2 | Installation of unauthorised software, tools, or applications | Act 854 s18 |
| 3 | Attempting to bypass, disable, or circumvent security controls | Act 854 s18; Computer Crimes Act 1997 |
| 4 | Accessing, downloading, distributing, or storing child sexual abuse material (CSAM) or any illegal content | Penal Code; Communications and Multimedia Act 1998 |
| 5 | Conducting or facilitating cyber attacks against any Organisation or third-party system | Act 854; Computer Crimes Act 1997 |
| 6 | Deploying malware, ransomware, spyware, or any malicious code | Computer Crimes Act 1997 |
| 7 | Intercepting or eavesdropping on network communications without authorisation | Computer Crimes Act 1997 |
| 8 | Exfiltrating sensitive, confidential, or customer data without authorisation | PDPA 2010; Act 854 |
| 9 | Accessing or using another person's credentials without their consent | Computer Crimes Act 1997 |
| 10 | Using Organisation systems to facilitate fraud, money laundering, or other criminal activities | Financial Services Act 2013; Penal Code |

### 6.2 General Prohibited Activities

The following activities are prohibited unless expressly authorised in writing by the CISO or a designated authority:

- **Unapproved data transfers:** Transmitting sensitive organisational data to personal email accounts, personal cloud storage, or other unapproved external destinations.
- **Unapproved software:** Downloading, installing, or using software not listed on the Organisation's **[Approved Software Register]**.
- **Cryptocurrency activities:** Mining cryptocurrency or conducting personal cryptocurrency transactions using Organisation resources.
- **Peer-to-peer file sharing:** Using peer-to-peer (P2P) network applications or BitTorrent clients on Organisation systems.
- **Proxies and anonymisation tools:** Using VPNs, proxy services, Tor, or anonymisation tools not provisioned by the Organisation.
- **Storing sensitive data locally:** Storing classified or sensitive data on local device storage, USB drives, or personal devices in violation of the Data Classification Policy.
- **Screen capture and recording:** Recording, screenshotting, or photographing sensitive or classified system screens without authorisation.
- **Disabling endpoint security:** Disabling, tampering with, or interfering with endpoint protection software, including antivirus, DLP agents, or MDM profiles.
- **Shadow IT:** Procuring, deploying, or using technology services outside the Organisation's formal IT governance process.
- **Excessive personal use:** Using Organisation systems for extended personal activities, including gaming, streaming, or operating a personal business.

### 6.3 Prohibited Content

Personnel must not create, access, store, transmit, or display the following categories of content on Organisation systems:

- [ ] Sexually explicit, pornographic, or obscene material
- [ ] Content that is threatening, harassing, defamatory, or discriminatory
- [ ] Content that violates the intellectual property rights of any party
- [ ] Content that incites racial, religious, or ethnic hatred
- [ ] Political campaigning or lobbying material unrelated to the Organisation's business
- [ ] Content that discloses Organisation trade secrets, confidential information, or customer data in an unauthorised manner
- [ ] Gambling or online gaming content [unless specifically exempted by role]

Personnel may use the Organisation's internet connection for:

## 9. Social Media Guidelines

*This section governs the use of social media in both personal and professional contexts where it may affect the Organisation or its regulatory standing.*

### 9.1 Personal Social Media Use During Work Hours

- Personnel must not access personal social media platforms on Organisation systems during working hours, except during designated break periods.
- Access to social media platforms on Organisation systems is subject to the content filtering controls described in Section 8.1.2.
- Excessive personal social media use that impacts productivity may result in disciplinary action.

### 9.2 Professional and Official Social Media

Personnel authorised to manage the Organisation's official social media presence must:

| Obligation | Detail |
|---|---|
| Obtain prior approval | Obtain written approval from **[Communications / Marketing / CISO]** before publishing content relating to the Organisation |
| Use designated accounts only | Post only from officially designated Organisation accounts |
| Comply with communication policies | Adhere to the Organisation's **[Communications and Media Policy]** |
| Report incidents | Immediately report any unauthorised access to or compromise of official social media accounts to the CISO |

### 9.3 Personal Social Media — Restrictions on Organisational Information

All personnel — regardless of whether they are using Organisation systems — must not:

- Disclose confidential, proprietary, or sensitive information about [Organization Name], its customers, transactions, systems, or security posture on any social media platform.
- Post content that could be construed as representing the official position of the Organisation without express authorisation.
- Share images, screenshots, or recordings of Organisation systems, premises, or operations.
- Engage in online activity that could damage the reputation of [Organization Name], its leadership, employees, or business partners.
- Disclose information about security incidents, vulnerabilities, regulatory matters, or investigations.

### 9.4 Social Engineering via Social Media

Personnel must be aware that threat actors may target them through social media platforms to extract information about the Organisation. Personnel must:

- Exercise caution when connecting with unknown individuals on professional networks (e.g., LinkedIn) who claim to work in related industries.
- Not disclose information about the Organisation's technology stack, security tools, or personnel structure in response to unsolicited social media inquiries.
- Report suspected social engineering attempts to the IT Security team.

### 9.5 Disclaimer Requirement

When discussing matters related to the financial services industry or regulatory environment in a personal capacity on social media, personnel should include a disclaimer such as:

> *"Views expressed are my own and do not represent the views of [Organization Name]."*

## 13. Policy Exceptions

*This section provides a controlled process for granting exceptions to policy requirements, preventing shadow IT while accommodating legitimate business needs.*

### 13.1 Exception Process

Exceptions to this Policy may be granted in exceptional circumstances where strict compliance would materially impede legitimate business operations. All exceptions must be:

1. Submitted via the **[IT Security Exception Request Form]** to the CISO.
2. Accompanied by a documented business justification and risk assessment.
3. Reviewed and approved in writing by the **CISO and [CTO/COO]**.
4. Time-limited to a maximum of **[12 months]**, after which renewal must be sought.
5. Recorded in the **Policy Exception Register** maintained by the IT Security team.
6. Reviewed in the context of the Organisation's annual policy review.

### 13.2 Exceptions Not Permitted

No exception will be granted for activities listed under Section 6.1 (Absolute Prohibitions). These prohibitions are absolute and non-negotiable.
