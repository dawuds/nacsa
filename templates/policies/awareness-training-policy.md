## 1. Purpose

This Policy establishes the mandatory cyber security awareness and training requirements for all personnel at [Organisation Name] who have access to National Critical Information Infrastructure (NCII) systems, networks, and data. It defines the obligations of the organisation and its workforce in maintaining a security-aware culture as a primary line of defence against cyber threats.

This Policy is issued in compliance with **Section 18 of the Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854)**, which places obligations on NCII entity operators to ensure that personnel responsible for or with access to NCII systems maintain the requisite cyber security competencies. It supplements [Organisation Name]'s overarching Information Security Policy and is read in conjunction with the Cyber Security Incident Response Plan and the Access Control Policy.

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 Organisational Scope

This Policy applies to [Organisation Name], including all business units, subsidiaries, and branch offices that operate, maintain, or have access to systems designated as NCII under the Cyber Security Act 2024.

### 2.2 Personnel Scope

This Policy applies without exception to:

- Temporary staff, secondees, and interns with access to NCII systems

This Policy covers all information systems, applications, networks, and data assets operated by [Organisation Name] that have been designated as NCII, or that process, store, or transmit data from NCII systems, as identified in the [Organisation Name] NCII Asset Register.

## 6. Role-Based Training Requirements

### 6.1 Policy Statement

Cyber security threats are not uniform across the organisation. Personnel with elevated access, technical responsibilities, or management authority over NCII systems require training that is proportionate to their exposure and responsibility. [Organisation Name] shall maintain and deliver a role-based training curriculum that addresses the specific risks and obligations of each defined role category.

### 6.2 Role Categories and Training Requirements

*Map each role category to its specific training topics. Authors should review and update this table during each annual Policy review cycle to reflect changes in technology, roles, and threat landscape.*

#### 6.2.1 General Personnel (All Staff)

All employees and contractors with any level of access to [Organisation Name] systems shall complete:

- Cyber security fundamentals and the threat landscape relevant to Malaysia
- Password hygiene and multi-factor authentication (MFA) usage
- Recognising and reporting phishing, social engineering, and suspicious activity
- Acceptable use of information systems and devices
- Data classification and handling requirements under PDPA and internal policy
- Physical security and clean desk policy
- Incident reporting procedures and escalation contacts

#### 6.2.2 IT and System Administrators

Personnel with administrative privileges over infrastructure, networks, or NCII systems shall additionally complete:

- Secure configuration and hardening of systems and networks
- Privileged access management and the principle of least privilege
- Patch and vulnerability management procedures
- Network monitoring and log management
- Secure remote access and VPN security
- NCII-specific technical controls as required by NACSA guidelines

#### 6.2.3 Information Security Personnel

Members of the security operations, risk, and compliance functions shall additionally complete:

- Threat intelligence analysis and application
- Security incident detection, analysis, and response
- Digital forensics and evidence handling fundamentals
- NCII regulatory obligations under the Cyber Security Act 2024
- Security assessment methodologies (vulnerability scanning, penetration testing governance)
- Relevant industry certifications as approved by the CISO (e.g., CISSP, CISM, CEH, CompTIA Security+)

#### 6.2.4 Software Developers and Application Teams

Personnel engaged in the design, development, or deployment of applications that interface with NCII systems shall additionally complete:

- Secure Software Development Lifecycle (SSDLC) principles
- OWASP Top 10 and common application vulnerabilities
- Secure coding practices for [relevant languages/frameworks in use]
- Code review and static/dynamic application security testing (SAST/DAST)
- API security and data handling within applications

#### 6.2.5 Senior Management and Board Members

Executives and board members shall complete:

- Cyber security governance and board-level accountability
- Regulatory obligations and penalties under the Cyber Security Act 2024 and PDPA
- Cyber risk and its translation into business impact
- Crisis communication and executive responsibilities during a cyber incident
- Emerging threats relevant to [Organisation Name]'s sector

#### 6.2.6 Third-Party Personnel

Vendors, contractors, and managed service providers with access to NCII systems shall complete:

- [Organisation Name] security policies and acceptable use requirements applicable to third parties
- Incident reporting obligations to [Organisation Name]
- Data handling and confidentiality requirements
- Physical and logical access security expectations

### 6.3 Role-Based Training Register

The CISO, in coordination with HR, shall maintain a **Role-Based Training Register** that maps each role to its required training modules, completion deadlines, and assessment requirements. The Register shall be reviewed and updated no less than annually.

## 7. Phishing Simulation Programme

### 7.1 Policy Statement

[Organisation Name] shall operate a continuous, risk-informed Phishing Simulation Programme to test and improve the organisation's resilience to social engineering attacks, which represent one of the primary vectors for compromise of NCII systems.

### 7.2 Programme Objectives

- Establish a measurable baseline of personnel susceptibility to phishing attacks
- Track improvement in click-through and reporting rates over time
- Identify personnel and business units requiring targeted intervention training
- Contribute evidence of proactive security culture to regulatory and audit bodies

### 7.3 Programme Governance

| Parameter | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Programme Owner** | CISO |
| **Operational Delivery** | [Security Operations / Managed Security Service Provider] |
| **Minimum Simulation Frequency** | [Quarterly] |
| **Scope** | All personnel within Policy scope, including third parties where contractually agreed |
| **Authorisation** | Each campaign requires CISO sign-off; no prior notice shall be given to recipients |
| **Tooling** | [Approved simulation platform, e.g., KnowBe4, Proofpoint, Cofense — specify] |

### 7.4 Campaign Design Standards

*Simulation campaigns must be designed to reflect realistic and current threat scenarios without causing undue disruption or reputational harm.*

- Simulations shall be based on real-world phishing techniques observed in the current threat environment
- Campaign templates shall vary in sophistication across the annual programme cycle to include basic, intermediate, and advanced scenarios
- Simulations shall not impersonate NACSA, BNM, PDRM, or any government authority in a manner that could constitute an offence
- All simulation data shall be treated as confidential and used solely for training and improvement purposes
- A debrief communication shall be issued to all targeted personnel following each campaign to reinforce learning

### 7.5 Metrics and Reporting

The following metrics shall be tracked and reported to the CISO and relevant governance committees on a [quarterly] basis:

| Metric | Description | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Susceptibility Rate | % of recipients who clicked a simulated phishing link | [< X%] |
| Credential Submission Rate | % of recipients who submitted credentials in simulation | [< X%] |
| Reporting Rate | % of recipients who reported the simulation via the correct channel | [> X%] |
| Remediation Completion Rate | % of failed recipients completing mandatory remediation training within SLA | [100%] |

### 7.6 Remediation and Follow-Up

Personnel who fail a phishing simulation (i.e., click a link, submit credentials, or fail to report a suspicious email) shall:

1. Be immediately redirected to a brief teachable moment explaining the simulation and correct behaviour
2. Be automatically enrolled in a targeted phishing awareness remediation module
3. Be required to complete the remediation module within [14] calendar days
4. Have the failure recorded in their training record for the purpose of risk-informed monitoring

Persistent failure (defined as [three or more] failures within a [12-month] period) shall be escalated to the individual's line manager and HR for further action in accordance with the [Organisation Name] HR Disciplinary Policy.

## 9. Competency Assessment

### 9.1 Policy Statement

[Organisation Name] shall assess the competency of all personnel following the completion of mandatory training to ensure that training objectives have been met. Assessment results shall be used to inform training programme improvements and to identify personnel requiring additional support.

### 9.2 Assessment Requirements

| Assessment Parameter | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Timing** | Assessments shall be completed within [5] business days of completing the corresponding training module |
| **Minimum Pass Mark** | [80%] for general awareness training; [75%] for role-based technical training (or as defined per module) |
| **Attempts Permitted** | Maximum of [3] attempts before mandatory escalation |
| **Format** | Multiple choice, scenario-based questions, or practical assessments as appropriate to the training type |
| **Record Retention** | Assessment results shall be retained for a minimum of [5] years |

### 9.3 Assessment Failure and Remediation

| Scenario | Action Required |
|---|---|
| First or second failed attempt | Personnel is required to retake the relevant training module and reassess |
| Third failed attempt | Escalation to line manager; CISO notified; personnel enrolled in targeted remediation session |
| Failure to attempt within deadline | Access restriction warning issued; non-compliance recorded in training register |

### 9.4 Competency Records

The CISO shall maintain, or ensure the maintenance of, a **Competency and Training Records Register** that captures for each individual in scope:

- Training modules assigned and completed
- Assessment scores and dates
- Pass/fail status
- Remediation actions taken
- Training compliance status (compliant / non-compliant / in progress)

This register shall be made available to NACSA or any authorised regulatory body upon request.

### 9.5 Organisational Reporting

## 10. New Joiner Induction Requirements

### 10.1 Policy Statement

All individuals joining [Organisation Name] or commencing engagement that grants access to organisational or NCII systems shall complete a mandatory cyber security induction as a condition of receiving system access. This requirement applies to permanent employees, contractors, and third-party personnel.

### 10.2 Induction Programme Requirements

### 10.2.1 Timing and Access Gating

*Access to NCII systems shall not be granted until the cyber security induction has been completed, or an exception has been formally approved by the CISO.*

| Personnel Category | Induction Deadline | System Access Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Permanent employee | Within [5] business days of start date | Full access contingent on completion of induction; limited access may be granted during the window |
| Contractor / Consultant | Prior to or on first day of engagement | Full access contingent on completion of induction |
| Third-party with remote NCII access | Prior to access being provisioned | Access provisioned only upon documented completion |
| Board / Executive | Within [30] days of appointment | Executive-tailored module to be completed |

### 10.2.2 Mandatory Induction Content

The cyber security induction module for new joiners shall at minimum cover:

- Overview of [Organisation Name]'s cyber security obligations under the Cyber Security Act 2024
- The significance of NCII designation and each employee's individual responsibility
- Organisational Information Security Policy and Acceptable Use Policy — key obligations and prohibited activities
- Password management, MFA setup, and identity management procedures
- Email security: recognising phishing, suspicious links, and how to report
- Data handling, classification, and PDPA obligations
- Incident reporting: what to report, how to report, and to whom
- Physical security expectations: clean desk, visitor management, physical access
- Consequences of non-compliance, including regulatory penalties applicable under Act 854
- Contact details for the CISO/Security Operations for queries and incident reporting

### 10.2.3 Acknowledgement and Sign-Off

Upon completing the induction module and associated assessment, each new joiner shall:

1. Complete and sign (digitally or physically) the **Cyber Security Awareness Acknowledgement Form** (Appendix A)
2. Confirm their understanding and acceptance of [Organisation Name]'s Information Security Policies
3. Acknowledge that their training and assessment record will be retained and may be reviewed by regulators

A copy of the signed acknowledgement shall be filed in the individual's HR record and the CISO's training register.

### 10.3 Ongoing Obligations

Completion of the induction module does not exempt a new joiner from subsequently completing the annual mandatory training cycle. New joiners who complete induction in [Month X] shall be enrolled in the next annual training cycle commencing from the following calendar year, unless directed otherwise by the CISO.

## 12. Non-Compliance and Enforcement

### 12.1 Policy Statement

Compliance with this Policy is mandatory. Failure to complete assigned training within prescribed deadlines, or failure to meet minimum assessment standards, constitutes a breach of [Organisation Name]'s security obligations and may constitute a contravention of obligations under the Cyber Security Act 2024.

### 12.2 Consequences of Non-Compliance

| Breach | First Occurrence | Repeated Breach |
|---|---|---|
| Failure to complete mandatory training by deadline | Formal reminder; line manager notified; extended deadline of [14] days | Formal warning under HR Disciplinary Policy; access restriction review |
| Failure to achieve minimum assessment pass mark after [3] attempts | Mandatory remediation session; line manager notified | Escalation to HR; formal disciplinary process initiated |
| Failure to complete phishing simulation remediation | Access restriction warning; line manager notification | Disciplinary review; restricted NCII system access pending completion |
| Failure to complete new joiner induction | NCII system access withheld until completion | HR notified; probationary review implications |

### 12.3 Regulatory Consequences

[Organisation Name] notes that obligations arising under Section 18 of the Cyber Security Act 2024 are enforceable by NACSA. Systemic failure to ensure personnel training may result in regulatory findings, NCII licence conditions, directives, or other enforcement actions against [Organisation Name] as an NCII entity. Personnel whose individual non-compliance contributes to a regulatory breach may face further consequences as determined by management and legal counsel.
