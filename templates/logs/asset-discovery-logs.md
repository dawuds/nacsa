## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This document serves as the official log template for automated asset discovery scans conducted across the National Critical Information Infrastructure (NCII) network environments of [Organization Name]. It provides a structured record of all assets identified, newly detected devices, anomalous or unauthorized endpoints, operating system and service fingerprinting results, network topology mappings, and Shadow IT discoveries arising from each scan cycle.

These logs support [Organization Name]'s obligations under the **Cyber Security Act 2024 (Act 854)**, specifically:

- **Section 17** — which mandates that NCII entities maintain accurate, up-to-date inventories of information assets forming part of the national critical information infrastructure; and
- **Section 18** — which requires NCII entities to implement proactive measures to detect, report, and respond to cybersecurity threats, including unauthorized devices and services operating within managed network environments.

### 1.2 Scope

These logs apply to all automated asset discovery scan activities covering:

- On-premises network segments and subnets designated as NCII environments
- Cloud-connected infrastructure segments within the [Organization Name] perimeter
- Operational Technology (OT) and Industrial Control System (ICS) network zones, where applicable
- Wireless network segments and guest network zones
- Any third-party or vendor-managed segments connected to [Organization Name] infrastructure

This log record is generated **per scan cycle** (minimum weekly frequency) and must be retained in accordance with [Organization Name]'s data retention policy and NACSA regulatory requirements.

### Appendix C — Rogue Device Classification Criteria

*Define the criteria used to classify a detected device as a rogue device for the purposes of this log.*

*[Organization Name] classifies a device as **rogue** if it meets one or more of the following criteria:*

- The device's MAC address is not present in the authorised asset register
- The device cannot be attributed to an approved procurement or provisioning record
- The device is operating on a restricted or sensitive network segment without documented authorisation
- The device is exhibiting behaviour inconsistent with its declared or inferred role (e.g., excessive port scanning, ARP poisoning)
- The device's hostname, OS fingerprint, or service banner does not match any known approved baseline profile

### Appendix D — Shadow IT Classification Criteria

*Define the criteria used to identify Shadow IT assets for the purposes of this log.*

*[Organization Name] classifies an asset or service as **Shadow IT** if it meets one or more of the following criteria:*

- A software application or cloud service is in active use by a business unit without formal IT approval or registration
- A hardware device is connected to the corporate network without an approved procurement and onboarding process
- A network service or workload is operating without a corresponding entry in the IT service catalogue
